Experience Kerala, explore 14 districts of God's own country

Wayand

The green paradise is nestled among the mountains of the Western Ghats, forming the border world of the greener part of Kerala. Clean and pristine, enchanting and hypnotizing,this land is filled with history and culture.

Kozhikode

Kozhikode has a unique place among other regions of Kerala. It was here that Vasco da Gama, the Portuguese navigator set foot in 1498 and is known for its beautiful beaches, historic monuments, unique culture and many more.
Alapuzha

Ringed by the azure Arabian Sea and the breezy backwaters on either sides, the sigh inducing Alappuzha resides in the central part of Godâ€™s Own Country as a picturesque patch of succulent green!
Idukki

Mist laden hills, enchanting valleys,cascades, rolling grasslands, sprawling plantations, colonial bungalows, wildlife and many more makes Idukki, a landlocked district in Central Kerala a unique destination for travellers.
Kottayam

Bordered by the lofty and mighty Western Ghats on the east and the Vembanad Lake and paddy fields of Kuttanad on the west.Panoramic backwater stretches, lush paddy fields, highlands, hills and hillocks, extensive rubber plantations.
Thiruvanathapuram

A city adorned with lush greenery, tranquil backwaters and exquisite beaches is Thiruvananthapuram, the capital city and cultural hub of God,s Own Country. This "Evergreen City of India" is located in the southwestern tip of Indian mainland.
Ernakulam

Ernakulam, the urbane face of Kochi, is a bustling place with crowded shopping malls, restaurants and high-rises. On the cultural, political, commercial and industrial fronts, Ernakulam is on the cutting edge, as far as the State is concerned.
Pathanamthitta

Situated in the southern region of Kerala, Pathanamthitta has nearly half of its total area under forest cover. It is the pilgrim center of Kerala, renowned for the shrine of Lord Ayappa in the Sabari Hills, drawing millions of devotees from near and far off places.
Kollam

Get back to nature with an adventure through Thenmala and Shendruney! Let the lazy Ashtamudi backwaters set the pace to your day and village folktales flow to your heart. Discover the dynamic festivals that leave imprints in your soul.

Kannur

Kannur is northernmost districts of Kerala blessed with full of greenary and historious places. Kannur is famous for Politics and Temple festivals. The region is famous for the dazzling ritual performance called Theyyam.

Thrishur

Known as the cultural capital of Kerala, the district of Thrissur is renowned worldwide for the magnificent festival of Pooram. It is also a center for learning some of the exquisite performing arts of Kerala as well as ancient Indian knowledge systems like Ayurveda.

Palakkad

It is often referred to as the rice granary of Kerala. It is the traditional gateway to Kerala from the north. Palakkad has a topography that is a blend of forest cover, rivers, valleys, hills etc. Palakkad has close cultural ties with the adjoining state of Tamilnadu.
Kasargode

This northernmost district of Kerala is famous for its coir and handloom industries and is also home to some exquisite folk arts and rituals of Kerala. The 300-year-old Bekal Fort is one of the main attractions of Kasaragod. Breathtaking backwater stretches and pristine beaches attract visitors to this district.

Malapuram

The hilly district of Malappuram falls among the northern districts in the state of Kerala. Closely bordered by the mountain ranges of the Western Ghats on its east, and flanked by the Arabian Sea on the West.It has an eventful history with the Zamorins of Kozhikode ruling the region for a significant period in the past.
